# Synthesized User Positioning Plan

## Goal

Position One Tap Headache Tracker Pro so users immediately understand why it is worth paying for.

The paid tier should not feel like a technical weather add-on. It should feel like the natural next step after logging headaches:

> Stop guessing what triggers your headaches. Find your patterns and get warned before risky days.

## Current Pro Offer

Pro currently unlocks:

- **Personalized Insights** — on-device analysis of logged headaches across sleep, pressure, time of day, weather, and other captured context.
- **Proactive Alerts** — background forecast checks that notify users before sharp pressure drops or air-quality spikes.
- **Custom alert settings** — pressure sensitivity, AQI threshold, and quiet hours.
- **Private local-first experience** — no accounts, no ads, no developer servers.
- **Purchase options** — yearly subscription with free trial and lifetime unlock.

## Core Positioning Shift

### Current implied positioning

> Pro gives you barometric pressure alerts, air-quality alerts, and pattern analytics.

### Recommended positioning

> Pro helps you stop guessing: find what your headaches have in common and get warned before risky days.

## Why This Shift Matters

Users are not primarily buying:

- Weather data
- AQI thresholds
- Charts
- Background refresh
- hPa calculations

They are buying:

- Fewer surprises
- More confidence
- A sense that their headache logs are finally useful
- Help preparing before a bad day
- A tracker that works without extra effort

## Target POVs

## POV1: The Simple Guy

### Mindset

This user saw the app in the App Store and thought:

> I get headaches. I want a simple tracker.

### What he values

- One-tap logging
- Easy setup
- Clear benefit
- No complicated health workflow
- Practical output he can understand quickly

### Risk

He may ignore Pro if it sounds too technical.

Terms that can create friction:

- Barometric pressure
- AQI
- Custom sensitivity
- Pattern analytics
- Forecast engine

### Message that works

> Know when a headache day might be coming.

### Paywall language for this user

- **Headline:** Know when a headache day might be coming
- **Subheadline:** Pro finds simple patterns in your headache history and gives you a heads-up before risky weather.
- **Feature framing:**
  - Find your likely triggers
  - Get warned before risky days
  - No extra journaling
  - Your data stays private

### App Store angle

Keep the main listing simple:

> Log headaches in one tap. Pro helps you find patterns and warns you before weather conditions that may trigger headaches.

## POV2: The Frustrated Person

### Mindset

This user has tried notes, spreadsheets, weather apps, migraine apps, or manual journaling.

They think:

> I keep tracking things, but nothing actually helps me.

### What they value

- Useful answers from their logged history
- Less guessing
- Less manual work
- Evidence they can use or discuss with a doctor
- Warnings before conditions get worse

### Risk

They will not pay for another passive logbook.

They need to believe Pro will convert their existing data into useful insight.

### Message that works

> Finally get something useful from your headache logs.

### Paywall language for this user

- **Headline:** Stop guessing what triggers your headaches
- **Subheadline:** Pro looks at your headache history and shows the patterns that keep repeating — then warns you before risky conditions are forecast.
- **Feature framing:**
  - See what your headaches have in common
  - Spot patterns around sleep, time, pressure, and weather
  - Get a heads-up before pressure drops or poor air quality
  - Use your own data, processed privately on your device

### Best conversion moment

The Insights tab is likely the strongest upgrade surface.

Use logged-count-aware copy:

- **0-2 headaches logged:** Keep logging. Pro gets more useful as your history grows.
- **3-6 headaches logged:** You are building a history. Pro can start looking for early patterns.
- **7+ headaches logged:** You have logged enough headaches for Pro to start showing what they have in common.

### App Store angle

> More than a headache log. Pro helps you understand what keeps showing up around your headaches and warns you before risky days.

## POV3: The Elder

### Mindset

This user wants help but does not want to fiddle with settings.

They think:

> I just want it to work. Do not make me configure a bunch of things.

### What they value

- Set-and-forget simplicity
- Trustworthy defaults
- Clear privacy promises
- No account
- No ads
- No complicated dashboard
- Lifetime purchase option

### Risk

They may feel overwhelmed by technical controls like hPa, AQI, sliders, thresholds, and forecast explanations.

### Message that works

> Set it once. Get helpful headache alerts.

### Paywall language for this user

- **Headline:** Set it once. Get helpful headache alerts.
- **Subheadline:** Pro can quietly check your local forecast and notify you when conditions may raise headache risk. Use the recommended settings or adjust them anytime.
- **Feature framing:**
  - Recommended settings included
  - Quiet hours so alerts do not wake you
  - Pay once option available
  - No account, no ads, no cloud headache history

### Settings recommendation

Make the default Pro Alerts experience feel safe and automatic.

Recommended structure:

1. Enable Proactive Alerts
2. Recommended settings summary
3. Quiet hours
4. Advanced settings
   - Pressure threshold
   - Air-quality threshold

The current controls can remain, but hPa and AQI should be secondary rather than the first thing users must understand.

### Lifetime

## Feature Priority for Conversion

Lead with this order:

1. **Personalized Insights** — strongest trust signal because it uses the user's own headache history.
2. **Proactive Alerts** — strong practical value once the user believes the app understands their patterns.
3. **No extra journaling** — reinforces the one-tap promise.
4. **Set-and-forget defaults** — reduces setup anxiety.
5. **Private by design** — builds trust for health-adjacent data.

## Implementation Priorities

### Priority 1: Rewrite paywall copy

Replace technical-first messaging with benefit-first messaging.

Use:

> Stop guessing what triggers your headaches

instead of:

> Get a heads-up before headache weather

### Priority 2: Improve locked Insights upsell

Make the Insights tab the primary conversion surface for frustrated users.

Add logged-count-aware copy when possible.

### Priority 3: Simplify Pro Alerts settings language

Keep technical controls available, but frame them as optional advanced settings.

### Priority 4: Update App Store metadata

Make Pro sound like the continuation of the free product:

Free = capture headaches easily.

Pro = understand and prepare.

### Priority 5: Emphasize lifetime option clearly

The lifetime product should reassure users who dislike subscriptions.

Use:

> Buy Lifetime — No Subscription

## Success Criteria

The Pro positioning is successful if a user can answer these questions within 5 seconds:

- What does Pro do for me?
- Why is it better than free?
- Do I need to do extra work?
- Can I trust it with my data?
- Can I avoid another subscription if I want?

Target answers:

- Pro helps me find headache patterns and warns me before risky days.
- Free logs headaches; Pro helps me understand and prepare.
- No, it uses what I already log.
- Yes, data stays on my device.
- Yes, lifetime purchase is available.

## Final Recommended Positioning

> Pro helps you stop guessing. It finds what your headaches have in common, warns you before risky days, and keeps everything private on your device.
