# c521 — User Experience Improvement Suggestions

A user-journey audit of **One Tap Headache Tracker** (iPhone, Watch, widget). Each item describes what someone is trying to do, what they feel today, and what should change — without prescribing implementation.

No code changes are implied here; this is a product/UX backlog tied to how people actually operate the app.

---

## How people use this app (mental model)

Most users fall into one of three modes:

| Mode | What they want | Success looks like |
|------|----------------|-------------------|
| **Crisis logger** | Tap once during pain; done in under 3 seconds | Event saved; optional “logged” confirmation; no guilt about missing details |
| **Pattern seeker** | After weeks of logs, understand triggers and get warnings | Clear path from 3→5→10 logs to “Patterns”; Pro feels like the answer to “why do I keep getting these?” |
| **Clinical sharer** | Export a timeline for a neurologist | History is trustworthy, readable, and exportable without learning CSV semantics |

Improvements below are grouped by those journeys, not by file or module.

---

## 1. First install — “Can I log yet?”

### 1.1 Onboarding ends before the user logs once

**User context:** Someone downloads the app during a headache or right after one. They want to tap **Log Headache** immediately.

**Current experience:** Three permission screens (welcome → Health → Location) with no live log on the last step. They only reach the big red button after finishing onboarding. Watch and widget taps before onboarding complete are rejected or deferred with a dialog telling them to open the app.

**Suggested change:** End onboarding with a single optional “Log your first headache now” on the final screen, or allow one “practice” log before permissions so the core promise (“one tap”) lands before system sheets. Make it obvious that permissions can be finished later without blocking the timestamp.

---

### 1.2 Severity and notes are invisible at first run

**User context:** A user who wants mild/medium/extreme tracking for doctor visits or pattern analysis.

**Current experience:** Onboarding never mentions severity. The toggle lives under **Settings → Logging → Prompt for severity and notes** (off by default). Users who never open Settings never discover it; severity insights in Patterns stay permanently unavailable with no explanation.

**Suggested change:** One lightweight onboarding beat: “Want to add severity after each tap? (optional)” with Skip / Yes. If skipped, surface a one-time card on Home after the third log: “Add severity in Settings to unlock severity patterns.”

---

### 1.3 Permission denial feels like a dead end

**User context:** User taps Continue on Health or Location but denies access in the system sheet.

**Current experience:** Onboarding still advances. Later, **Latest Event** shows orange badges (“Health: unavailable”, “Environment: unavailable”) and a recovery card with technical status text. **Settings → Permissions** shows generic summaries but not “denied — tap to fix.”

**Suggested change:** When capture is partial, replace engineer-facing status strings with plain language: “Location off — weather won’t attach until you enable it in Settings.” Link directly to the relevant Settings pane. On Home, show one persistent “Improve your logs” strip until both are granted or the user dismisses with “I’m fine with time-only logs.”

---

### 1.4 First session can stack multiple Pro pitches

**User context:** New user completes onboarding, logs once, then explores tabs.

**Current experience:** Multiple independent triggers can appear in one session: **Pro intro** (~0.8s after launch for legacy users), **trial offer** (~4s after first log or ~3s for existing users with logs), **milestone Pro card** on Home at 3/5/10 logs, and a **second trial touch** when opening Patterns. Flags reduce repeats across sessions but the first day can still feel like a sales funnel.

**Suggested change:** Cap to **one** monetization moment per session with a clear priority (e.g. first-log trial OR milestone, never both within 10 minutes). After dismissal, wait until the user has a “success moment” (e.g. fifth log or first export) before the next pitch.

## 2. Core loop — logging during a headache

### 2.1 The primary button state is easy to misread

**User context:** User taps **Log Headache** and needs to know: saved vs still working vs failed.

**Current experience:** Button disables while capturing; subtitle switches to “Saving and collecting context…”; then a 3.5s “Logged” overlay. Banner text cycles (“Context saved”, “Saved with partial context”, etc.) but competes with the card below. **Undo** and **Add Details** only appear while `lastCapturedEventID` is set — easy to miss if they scroll.

**Suggested change:** Keep one unambiguous status line under the button until the user dismisses it or starts a new log: “Saved · Health ✓ · Weather ✓” or “Saved · Weather missing.” Make **Undo** a short snackbar (“Undo log”) for 10–15 seconds so it survives scrolling.

---

### 2.2 Widget log feels successful before context exists

**User context:** User logs from the home screen widget without opening the app.

**Current experience:** Widget shows “Logged” for ~10s; Siri/dialog says “Headache logged.” Row is stored immediately; Health and weather stay pending until the main app foregrounds. User may never open the app and assume the rich CSV columns are already filled.

**Suggested change:** Widget confirmation copy: “Logged — open app to add health & weather” (or a subtle pending indicator on the widget until enrichment completes). When enrichment runs on open, a single banner: “Finished updating 2 widget logs from earlier.”

---

### 2.3 Watch says “Logged” when the phone did the work

**User context:** Migraine at night; phone is across the room; Watch is the only device in hand.

**Current experience:** Watch sends to iPhone; green **Logged** on success even if iPhone is locked, permissions missing, or enrichment fails. Queued state says “Queued — will sync when iPhone is nearby” then status text fades (~4s). No persistent queue count. Early “Connecting to iPhone…” with no retry affordance if session isn’t ready.

**Suggested change:** Distinguish **“Sent to iPhone”** vs **“Saved with full context.”** Show a small badge when N logs are queued. If iPhone unreachable after timeout, offer **“Log anyway (time only)”** on Watch for users who accept incomplete rows rather than losing the moment.

---

### 2.4 Duplicate logs erode trust in History

**User context:** User reviews timeline before a doctor appointment.

**Current experience:** Rare WatchConnectivity paths can deliver the same tap twice; History shows duplicate timestamps with no “likely duplicate” hint.

**Suggested change:** Soft dedupe in UI (collapse same-minute entries with “Possible duplicate — merge?”) or dedupe on ingest using `requestID` from the watch payload.

---

## 3. After the tap — “What did the app actually record?”

### 3.1 Latest Event card speaks developer, not human

**User context:** User glances at Home to see if last night’s log is “good enough” for Patterns.

**Current experience:** Badges like `Complete`, `Health: captured`, `Environment: captured` and chips for Steps / Sleep / AQI. Recovery card can show raw messages (“Health unavailable — …”).

**Suggested change:** Lead with a one-line human summary: “Tuesday 9:14 PM · Evening · 72°F, pressure falling · slept 6.2h.” De-emphasize raw status enums; tuck technical detail behind “Why is something missing?”

---

### 3.2 Partial capture recovery is scary during pain

**User context:** User already tapped once; capture failed partially.

**Current experience:** Orange **CaptureRecoveryCard** with **Try Again** and **Email Developer**. Helpful for support, heavy for a patient.

**Suggested change:** Default action: **Try Again** only. Move **Email Developer** under “Still stuck?” Keep pre-filled email for power users, not as equal weight to retry.

---

### 3.3 “What Gets Captured” is always on Home

**User context:** Daily user who has logged 50+ times.

**Current experience:** Long educational block (time, Health, weather) remains on every visit to **One Tap**, pushing recent entries down.

**Suggested change:** Collapse after N logs or first successful full capture; replace with a single “How logging works” link in Settings. Reserve Home for action + latest + recent.

---

## 4. History — reviewing and sharing with a doctor

### 4.1 Empty History doesn’t teach the primary action

**User context:** User opens **History** before logging, looking for “how this works.”

**Current experience:** `ContentUnavailableView` points to the One Tap tab — good — but optional quiz card competes for attention on empty state.

**Suggested change:** Empty state hierarchy: (1) illustration + “Tap Log Headache on One Tap”, (2) optional quiz as secondary link, not a large card above the fold.

---

### 4.2 Export is powerful but buried in cognitive load

**User context:** User needs a CSV before an appointment tomorrow.

**Current experience:** Export lives in list footer **and** toolbar menu; year filter affects export count; user must understand filtered vs all years.

**Suggested change:** When year filter is active, export button label: **“Export 12 entries (2025)”** with subcopy “or export all years.” After export, brief confirmation: “CSV ready — share to Files, Mail, or your doctor.”

---

### 4.3 Import is correct but intimidating

**User context:** User restores from backup or merges an old phone export.

**Current experience:** Intro sheet → file picker → skip/overwrite strategy alert with row counts. Errors use `localizedDescription`.

**Suggested change:** Plain-language preview: “Found 84 headaches from Jan 2024–Mar 2026. 3 duplicates — Skip or Replace?” Show one example row (date + city) so they trust the parser.

---

### 4.4 Editing an entry is discoverable only by tapping a row

**User context:** User needs to fix severity or notes days later.

**Current experience:** Tap row → **EditEventSheet**. No hint on rows that severity is empty.

**Suggested change:** Subtle “Add severity” on rows missing it; after edit, toast “Updated — Patterns refresh on next open.”

---

### 4.5 Swipe-delete has no safety net

**User context:** User accidentally swipes while scrolling a long list.

**Current experience:** Standard list delete with no undo.

**Suggested change:** Confirm delete for entries older than 7 days, or offer 5-second undo snackbar.

---

## 5. Patterns tab — from curiosity to Pro

### 5.1 Tab name vs navigation title mismatch

**User context:** User remembers “Patterns” from marketing but sees **Patterns** tab and mixed copy (“See what triggers your headaches”, “Headache Pro”).

**Current experience:** Tab label **Patterns**; some strings still say “insights” in code comments; Pro learning states mix product name and feature name.

**Suggested change:** Pick one user-facing word (**Patterns** recommended) everywhere: tab, paywall bullets, notifications, Settings row.

---

### 5.2 Free user sees price only after commitment

**User context:** Price-sensitive user opens Patterns to see if Pro is worth it.

**Current experience:** Locked teaser with sample or blurred real preview; bottom CTA **See Pro plans** with log count. No price until paywall sheet.

**Suggested change:** Show starting price on the teaser footer (“From $X/year · 7-day trial”) when StoreKit products are loaded. If trial ineligible, say “Trial used on this Apple ID — $X/year.”

---

### 5.3 Buying Pro before having five logs feels like a bait-and-switch

**User context:** User pays after the History upsell (“enough for patterns”) with only 4 logs.

**Current experience:** Pro unlocks but Patterns shows progress “3 of 5 logs” and example cards — alerts work, patterns don’t yet.

**Suggested change:** On paywall and History upsell, explicit gate: **“Patterns unlock at 5 logs (you have 4). Alerts work immediately.”** Consider soft-locking purchase until 5 logs *or* frame Pro as “Alerts now + patterns as you log.”

---

### 5.4 Sample insights don’t feel like *their* data

**User context:** User with 0–4 logs opens Patterns.

**Current experience:** Generic sample rows (“40% of headaches in the evening”) regardless of actual count. Users with 4 logs see the same fiction as users with 0.

**Suggested change:** Under 5 logs, show a **progress-first** screen: “Log 2 more headaches to unlock your first pattern” with a preview skeleton, not fake percentages.

---

### 5.5 Pro user with 5+ logs but no signal hits a dead end

**User context:** User paid; logs religiously; no factor crosses statistical threshold.

**Current experience:** “Still watching for a clear pattern” with example card — better than empty, but no next steps.

**Suggested change:** Actionable guidance: “Log at different times of day,” “Enable severity in Settings,” “Check Health permissions,” “Give it 2 more weeks.” Optionally show “near miss” factors (e.g. humidity 38% vs 40% threshold) in an “Almost visible” section.

---

### 5.6 Insight detail is read-only and long

**User context:** User wants to understand “Most common day: Tuesday” and decide what to do.

**Current experience:** Static chart, **Your pattern**, **Why this matters**, optional alerts CTA, disclaimer — long scroll, no tap-on-bar values.

**Suggested change:** Interactive chart (tap bar → exact count/%). Sticky footer CTA when insight is weather-related: **“Turn on pressure alerts.”** Short “TL;DR” line at top: “Tuesdays: 4 of 11 headaches (36%).”

---

### 5.7 Proactive Alerts card appears even when irrelevant

**User context:** User’s only insight is weekday or time-of-day; they don’t care about barometric alerts.

**Current experience:** **Proactive Alerts** promo block in Patterns list and on insight detail for pressure/AQI-linked categories only on detail — but list header still pushes alerts broadly.

**Suggested change:** Show alerts upsell only when the user has a pressure or AQI insight, or when personal alert profile shows a supported signal. Otherwise hide or replace with “Alerts aren’t relevant to your top pattern yet.”

---

### 5.8 Headache quiz promise vs delivery gap

**User context:** User completes optional quiz from History (“helps Patterns surface what matters”).

**Current experience:** Quiz stores answers; connection to which insights appear is not obvious in the UI.

**Suggested change:** After quiz completion, show one concrete outcome: “We’ll prioritize sleep-related patterns” or “Thanks — your answers inform alert copy.” If quiz doesn’t affect engine yet, soften History copy to “Helps us improve future versions” until it does.

---

## 6. Proactive alerts — “Warn me before bad days”

### 6.1 Setup is a form, not a guided flow

**User context:** New Pro subscriber wants notifications without reading hPa documentation.

**Current experience:** **Settings → Proactive Alerts** (or Patterns card) opens a Form: toggles, sliders in hPa, AQI stepper, quiet hours, test button, personalization rows with “supported / not yet.”

**Suggested change:** First-run wizard: (1) Enable notifications, (2) “Your logs suggest pressure matters — use default 4 hPa?”, (3) Send test alert. Keep advanced sliders under **Customize.**

---

### 6.2 Users don’t know if alerts are “working” day to day

**User context:** User enabled alerts a week ago; no notification yet.

**Current experience:** **Last proactive alert** date in config; footer explains forecast checks. No “last checked forecast” or “nothing risky this week.”

**Suggested change:** Status line: “Last checked today 6:12 AM — no alert needed” vs “Last alert: Tue — pressure drop.” Reduces anxiety that background refresh is broken.

---

### 6.3 Alert copy may be too clinical when it fires

**User context:** User receives notification during commute.

**Current experience:** Notifications include probability lift and breakdown (valuable but dense).

**Suggested change:** Lock screen: one plain sentence (“Pressure may drop 5 hPa tonight — on days like yours, headaches were more common”). Detail on tap in app.

---

### 6.4 Trial purchase cancel feedback improved — extend to paywall

**User context:** User starts trial from animated sheet, cancels Apple sheet.

**Current experience:** Trial sheet shows “Trial wasn’t started. Tap again…” — good. RevenueCat **PaywallView** paths may still feel abrupt on success (sheet dismiss only).

**Suggested change:** On successful Pro unlock: 3-second **“You’re on Pro”** moment listing what unlocked (Alerts + Patterns), then land on Patterns or Alerts config with one highlighted next step.

---

## 7. Settings & trust — long-term maintenance

### 7.1 Restore purchases in Settings now feedback — keep parity everywhere

**User context:** User reinstalls app or switches phones.

**Current experience:** Settings **Restore Purchases** shows alert with success or “No previous purchase…” — fixed compared to older audits. Paywall restore should behave identically.

**Suggested change:** Same copy and spinner pattern on hosted paywall restore; never silent.

---

### 7.2 Health row doesn’t reflect authorization truth

**User context:** User denied Health read; wonders if Patterns are broken.

**Current experience:** **Apple Health** row shows “Available on this device” — not authorization state.

**Suggested change:** Show **Connected / Not connected / Denied** with action to open Health app permissions.

---

### 7.3 Appearance and units are fine; logging prefs need prominence

**User context:** User wants Celsius and severity prompts.

**Current experience:** Display section is clear; logging toggle is one row among Pro, permissions, about.

**Suggested change:** When `promptForSeverityNotes` is off but user has 5+ logs without severity, badge on Settings tab or inline hint under **Logging**.

---

## 8. Cross-surface consistency

### 8.1 Four entry points, one mental model

**User context:** User mixes iPhone, Watch, widget, Siri.

**Current experience:** Same event model, different confirmation semantics and enrichment timing.

**Suggested change:** Document in-app once (**Settings → How logging works**): table of surfaces → what’s captured when → “always opens iPhone eventually for weather/Health.”

---

### 8.2 Tab bar labels vs user vocabulary

**User context:** App Store says “headache logger”; tab says **One Tap**.

**Current experience:** Four tabs: One Tap, History, Patterns, Settings.

**Suggested change:** Usability test **Log** vs **One Tap** vs **Home**. Consider **Log** for the primary tab to match action language.

---

### 8.3 Doctor export and in-app units

**User context:** US user exports for US doctor; app shows Celsius if toggled.

**Current experience:** CSV includes both unit systems per README; in-app display respects toggle.

**Suggested change:** Export confirmation: “CSV includes metric and imperial columns.” Optional export preset: “US clinical” vs “Metric.”

## 10. What already improved (don’t regress)

These areas show intentional UX work in the current tree — future changes should preserve the behavior:

- **Watch cold start:** Queued tap dates replay when the phone handler attaches (`PhoneWatchSession.pendingTapDates`).
- **Widget enrichment:** Pending rows backfilled on foreground with user-visible banner count.
- **Trial offer:** Cancel/error copy on direct trial sheet; compliant price disclosure when `directPurchase` is true.
- **Patterns teaser:** Real insight preview when user has ≥5 logs and non-empty summary (not only hardcoded samples).
- **Pro learning state:** Separates “alerts ready now” vs “patterns need N logs” for paying users.
- **Restore in Settings:** Spinner + alert message after restore.
- **History sheet race:** Single `activeSheet` enum for export vs edit.

---

## Summary

The product’s promise — **one tap, rich context, private patterns** — is strong. The biggest UX gaps are not missing features but **moments of uncertainty**: after a tap (was it saved?), after a widget/watch log (is context complete?), before Pro purchase (what do I get at my current log count?), and when Patterns stay empty (what should I do next?). Improvements should tighten those moments in plain language, reduce competing monetization surfaces in session one, and align every surface (phone, watch, widget) to the same honest status story.
