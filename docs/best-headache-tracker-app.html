Headache Logger

Best for: people who want maximum data with minimum effort.

Headache Logger takes a fundamentally different approach. Instead of asking you questions, it captures context automatically. One tap logs a headache and simultaneously pulls in 60+ data points from Apple Health, local weather and barometric pressure, air quality, and pollen levels.

The trade-off is that it doesn't capture subjective data like pain intensity, location on head, or specific symptoms. If you need those, pair it with notes or use a different tracker. But if your goal is finding environmental and physiological triggers through data, nothing else captures as much context with as little effort.

Free, no ads, no account, no cloud. Learn more

Migraine Buddy

Best for: detailed subjective symptom logging with community features.

Migraine Buddy is the most popular migraine tracker, with a large user community and detailed logging for pain intensity, duration, location, symptoms, medications, and potential triggers. The trade-off is that each entry requires filling out a multi-step form, which can be difficult during severe episodes.

The free tier includes ads and limits some features. Premium ($9.99/month) unlocks PDF/CSV reports, weather correlation, and removes ads. Requires an account with cloud sync.

Bearable

Best for: tracking headaches alongside other symptoms and mood.

Bearable is a general-purpose symptom tracker that handles headaches alongside mood, sleep, medications, diet, and other factors. It's highly customizable and offers good Apple Health integration. The multi-symptom approach is powerful if you're tracking more than just headaches.

The free tier is functional but limited. Premium ($7.99/month) unlocks correlations, insights, and data export. No Apple Watch app.

N1 Headache

Best for: clinical-grade headache classification and medication tracking.

N1 Headache uses the International Classification of Headache Disorders (ICHD-3) criteria to help classify headache types. Strong medication tracking with dosage and efficacy logging. Good for people working closely with a neurologist on diagnosis.

Steeper learning curve. Pro features ($4.99/month) unlock additional analysis and export options. No Apple Watch or widget support.

Which One Should You Use?

  • Want maximum data, minimum effort? Headache Logger. One tap, 60+ auto-captured columns, free.
  • Want detailed subjective logging? Migraine Buddy. More work per entry, but captures pain quality, symptoms, and medications.
  • Tracking more than headaches? Bearable. Multi-symptom tracking with mood and lifestyle factors.
  • Need clinical headache classification? N1 Headache. ICHD-3 criteria, medication efficacy tracking.

There's no reason you can't use more than one. Headache Logger captures the objective context automatically; a subjective tracker can capture how the headache felt. Together, you get the full picture.
